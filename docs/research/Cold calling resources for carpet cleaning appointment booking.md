## Sales frameworks that work for local service appointment booking

### The "Brief Introduction to Book" framework (Method Clean Biz)

This is the most proven framework specifically for carpet cleaning cold calls:

1. Call and ask for the decision-maker by role ("the person who handles carpet cleaning")
2. Give a brief intro — name, company, specialization — under 15 seconds
3. Deliver a one-liner value proposition tied to a pain point
4. Ask to "stop by for a brief 10-minute inspection and come up with a couple of solutions"
5. If they're not ready, get their email and start a multi-touch follow-up campaign

The entire philosophy: **you are not selling — you are introducing yourself to set up an on-site estimate.** This framing dramatically reduces resistance.

### Gong.io's data-backed cold call structure

Based on analysis of **90,000+ cold calls**, the highest-converting structure is: open with your full name and company → say **"How have you been?"** (6.6x more likely to book a meeting) → state **"The reason for my call is..."** (2.1x more successful) → deliver a concise value hook → use an interest CTA like _"Does it make sense for me to give you more detail about how we do that?"_ rather than immediately asking for a specific meeting time. Critical finding: **"Did I catch you at a bad time?"** makes you 40% less likely to book a meeting — avoid it.

### Sandler Selling System's Pain Funnel

Focus on discovering the prospect's pain, not pushing your services. Use "Upfront Contracts" to set expectations: _"We haven't spoken before, and I'll be honest — this is a sales call. If you'd give me 15 seconds to share why I'm calling, you can decide if it's worth continuing."_ Then use probing questions to uncover dissatisfaction with their current situation. The key insight: **the first call's goal is to gather qualifying information, not to close.**

### Pattern Interrupt technique

Say something unexpected to jolt the prospect out of their automatic "reject the salesperson" response. Examples adapted for Bulldog Carpet Cleaning: _"I'll be honest — this is a cold call. Mind if I take 27 seconds to tell you why I reached out?"_ (the odd number creates a pause), or _"I'm sure you probably already have a carpet cleaning provider — most businesses in the area do. I'm actually not calling to replace them..."_ This pre-empting strategy (sometimes called "8-Miling") disarms the most common objection before it's raised.

### SPIN Selling for carpet cleaning

Developed from 35,000+ sales call studies, SPIN uses four question types to guide prospects toward recognizing their own need: **Situation** ("Do you currently have a dedicated carpet cleaning program?"), **Problem** ("What challenges do you have keeping high-traffic areas clean?"), **Implication** ("How does carpet appearance affect your tenants' impression of the building?"), and **Need-Payoff** ("Would it help to have a maintenance program that keeps everything looking fresh year-round?"). This framework is ideal for prospects who don't yet realize they have a carpet maintenance problem.

## How to handle the five most common objections

### "We already have a carpet cleaner"

This is the #1 objection. The expert consensus across all sources: **don't fight it — get curious.** Method Clean Biz recommends: _"Oh, OK. Well, as I talk with other facility managers, I notice they often express challenges with keeping their carpets spot-free and maintaining high-traffic areas. What are you most concerned with?"_ An alternative pre-emptive approach: _"I figured you probably do — most businesses in the area have someone. I'm actually not calling to replace them. I just wanted to see if there's a specific challenge they haven't been able to solve, like high-traffic areas or recurring stains."_ For Bulldog, the **100% soap-free, chemical-free process** is a natural differentiator here — most competitors can't match it.

### "Not interested"

Gong.io's research shows most "not interested" responses are **knee-jerk reactions, not real objections.** Use the validate-label-ask framework: _"Totally fair. Before I let you go, can I ask just one question? When was the last time your carpets were professionally cleaned?"_ This re-engages with a problem-awareness question. Another option: _"I completely understand. Just so I know — is it the topic that isn't relevant, or is it just bad timing?"_

### "Send me information / email me"

Method Clean Biz treats this as a **win, not a loss**: _"Absolutely, what email should I send it to?"_ This captures their email for your follow-up campaign. Then add: _"I'll send some information with a couple of case studies. Would it be OK if I followed up in a few days?"_ Given that **80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups**, keeping the door open is the real objective.

### "How much does it cost?"

Never quote a price on the phone. Redirect to the appointment: _"Great question. It really depends on your space — square footage, carpet type, and traffic patterns. That's actually why I'd love to stop by for a quick 10-minute walkthrough. I can give you an accurate quote on the spot and a couple of options. When would work?"_ The need for an in-person assessment **is** the reason for the free estimate — use it.

### "We don't have the budget right now"

Validate, then plant a seed: _"I completely understand. Many businesses we work with start with a quarterly maintenance program rather than a big one-time expense. Can I send you some info on how we help other offices keep costs manageable? And if it's OK, I'll follow up in about 90 days."_ This positions Bulldog for the future while capturing the email today.
